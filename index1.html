Instructor's Stance

I think for poetry— it is important to read aloud and hear recitations since poetry has an element of musicality. Therefore, students will be asked to read aloud in class and also grow a habit of hearing poems online. This will help them to understand the words and their relations better, and consequently make more sense of the message that the poets convey. Traditional methods of learning such as exams and home tasks will be given as well new trends which include active participation and group works will be there too.

Chronicles of a Soul's Nocturnal Voyage

This poem is based on my life journey and my identity's changes. Its narrative structure and rhythmic, rhyming language resemble poetry. The verses depict a happy, dreamy past with a strong faith in a higher power, set against a tight-knit hometown and faithful friends. Moving to an unknown city symbolises a major transformation that changed my goals and viewpoint. My character changes during the next four years of love and hate. In the city's hustle, loneliness creeps in and optimism fades. The poem explores losing ambitions, plans, and a new self created by unexpected events. It references a longing for the past and laments life's turbulence not preparing the soul for disintegration. Figurative language like "faithful flame within" and "loneliness a distant host," lends passion and depth to the story. The poem prompts readers to consider their own lives and the universal themes of change, grief, and resilience.
